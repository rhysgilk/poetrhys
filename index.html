washed away

i started doing a skin care routine each night
wash my face
cleanse my scalp
lotion, moisturizer
eye cream, face mist
and from that moment, i keep my glasses off
and lay on the bed
with my dimmable lights on low
and let the cool amber fog
creep into the corners of my room
just barely strong enough to lift up
the dark light settling in
and my fan is also set to low
which brings life to a gentle manatee
gliding across my sheets
and caressing my arms and waist
she hums a familiar song whose melody
push and pull the fabric of the room
and stretch at the hem
when was the last time you paused life
and started ripping away at the fabric
this little Wind did
and the manatee got away

a room so overgrown it became a jungle

i covered my wall with vines
and arranged orchids and thistles
and pinks ones i don’t remember the names of
albeit artificial, both vines and perennials
when i glance past my desk and out the glazed glass
—shattered towards the right
from when i tried to wash away the stains—
and green fills my peripheral
i feel like i’m in the rainforest
and i turn on my fan to a low speed
to feel the breeze graze my neck
and even my little hairs start to sway
as they bask and they breath

i only chose to install the curtains recently
to block out the moon’s glow while i sleep
although i have no trouble switching with
such a glow in my eyes
and i have an eye mask if needed
rather, to preserve my vast planetarium
and its wonders and smells and hues

all of my furniture this time around is made from
light woods—oak, pine, maple
and white accents with lamps
and drawers and bookshelves with more foliage
at this point i wonder if i
should just break the whole window
who needs inside or out
there shouldn’t be sides at all
just the Wind dancing as she pleases
and her audience admiring her gown

ferry

carried from bank and bending
to ride on the rolling roar
rocked by the river rapids
and kissed by the jagged floor

chaos brought forth by nature
for means of travel merry
magic as nymph or pixie
that’s why it’s called a ferry

moth

today i became a moth
i shed my old skin and left it behind
what lies between the broken walls
and under the falling stars
my old hopes and dreams
hidden from those who didn’t believe
and thus hidden from myself
but i carry a map, mostly unmarked
should i decide to return

firework

everything is born from darkness
which stretches into the great beyond indefinitely
until a singular point dots that infinite canvas
beginning a chain reaction of
unrelenting blasts of shimmering wonder
a depth of anecdotes and ideas
piercing from that smoke covered cloak
reaching our eyes
glittered grey of billions of sparkling stories
our eyes must pick and choose
which not to ignore
as too much color and too much light
may lead to a blind eye
and as the explosions fade into time
we are left with the after light
shadows etched into the memories of our eyes
which continue to imagine
the stories beyond the end
falling down past the horizon
and dying the the ground from which she came

fantasy

the difference between humans and fairies
is that humans take from this planet
while fairies give back to it

that must be why we can’t see them anymore
i don’t blame them for hiding from us
so that we may not steal their magic

i love the way your golden grains glow

i didn’t used to like the beach
the sun burned my eyes
and the sand burned my soles

the coast hides sharp rocks
and seaweed tangles and enwraps your limbs
dragging you down and in with the tide
and unlike the other kids,
i was afraid to breath under water
because when the salt touched my nose
i felt like a boiling quahog

i didn’t like how many people were there
and how people stared
and i didn’t like my body

and somehow
no matter how diligently you rinse off
before getting in the car
sand finds its way back home and settles
into the carpet threads or under the baseboards
awaiting a meal or the heating to turn on
to keep warm
it misses the ocean and the seagulls and the shells

nowadays i like to lay in the Wind
and listen to the sand tell me
thousands of stories in its thousands of lifetimes

daisy

she told me how she loves spring
and how much she loves the sun
and the shade from a great wind tree
and the night time
and the days in between
how she loved thursdays because that’s when
the children come skipping around
with their little metal lunch boxes
filled with juices and apple slices
and cheese and carrots and sometimes
yogurt and sometimes crackers
and they all sit down on the fresh mulch
and devour their little lunches
she loves the bees
and even though i do not care for them
i will try to since she insists that their wings
create music that puts her to sleep
and the pollen they carry smell like
fields of mowed grass and dew
and they give life to all most importantly
i wish i could see the life from your bud

beneath the transmission tower

just how many stars are in the sky
is not something i thought before that night
when i laid in the wild grass and let my body
be a beacon for not just ants and mosquitos

between the shutter of my eyelids
i let whatever wonders of life that we call light
reach my eyes and sketch their stories onto mine
while damp ground beneath me absorbed my shell

i faltered not whilst beholding
each and every delight i could catch
with my eyelashes and pray
that they won’t escape

i thought back to the last time i thunk
and could not recall a time where the only axiom
was one life with infinite pleasure that has been
split and divided amongst the cosmos

but in those moments as my soiled body
was drained of nutrients
i transaw a network of life
and became a beacon of light
